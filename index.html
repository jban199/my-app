Gods

Shiva

Beej mantra: ‡•ê (aum)

Shiva: The God of Destruction. Shiva is one of the most supreme deities in the Hindu religion. The name "Shiva" means "Auspicious One," signifying this deity's prominence. Hinduism is an ancient and diverse religion, so some sects of the faith hold Shiva to be the most important god, while others do not.

Vishnu

Beej mantra: Kleem

In Hinduism, Vishnu is a principal deity, considered the preserver and protector of the universe, and a member of the Trimurti (Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva) alongside the creator and the destroyer.

View

Kaali

Beej mantra: Kreem

In Hinduism, Kali is a goddess associated with time, death, and doomsday, also known as the "dark goddess" (or "black goddess"). She is also considered a powerful symbol of feminine energy, destruction, and creation.

View

Bramha

Beej mantra: Kham

In Hinduism, Brahma is the creator god, part of the Trimurti (Hindu Trinity) alongside Vishnu (the preserver) and Shiva (the destroyer), and is associated with creation, knowledge, and the Vedas.

View

Saraswati

Beej mantra: Aim

Saraswati is a prominent Hindu goddess revered as the deity of knowledge, education, learning, arts, speech, music, and poetry. She is also known as the personification of the sacred river Saraswati and is often depicted with a white lotus, a veena (a stringed instrument), and books.

View

Lakshmi

Beej mantra: Shreem

Lakshmi is a prominent Hindu goddess, the wife of Vishnu, and is revered as the goddess of wealth, good fortune, beauty, and prosperity. She is also part of the Tridevi, a trinity of goddesses along with Parvati and Saraswati.
